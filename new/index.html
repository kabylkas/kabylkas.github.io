Intern → Senior Software Engineer → Member of Technical Staff

2021 - 2026
Advanced Micro Devices (AMD)

I began my work at AMD as an intern and later continued as a remote contractor (Senior Software Engineer) through Luxoft, before transitioning into a Member of Technical Staff role. Throughout this progression, I have developed and maintained AMD’s GPU machine-readable ISA documentation, a comprehensive set of XML specifications describing AMD’s latest GPU ISAs. These specifications define instructions, encodings, operands, data formats, and human-readable descriptions.

I also develop and maintain the AMD GPU IsaDecoder API, a C++ library that enables efficient parsing of these XML specifications and decoding of individual instructions as well as entire shaders. The tool supports multiple AMD architectures, including CDNA™ 3, CDNA™ 2, RDNA™ 3, and RDNA™ 2, and provides developers with a practical and scalable way to analyze and interact with GPU ISAs.

This work is open source. You can explore the project on GPUOpen https://gpuopen.com/machine-readable-isa/ and on GitHub https://github.com/GPUOpen-Tools/isa_spec_manager.

Assistant Professor

2023 - 2026
Nazarbayev University, Astana

As an Assistant Professor at Nazarbayev University, I teach C/C++ and Computer Systems courses and lead Maveric@NU, the Micro Architecture Verification Center. At Maveric, we advance hardware verification through innovative research and by developing more efficient methods to keep pace with the growing complexity of modern microarchitectures.

In addition to research and education, our team actively designs microprocessor chips in Kazakhstan, taking ideas from architecture and verification all the way to silicon. These efforts have resulted in the successful fabrication of microchips designed locally, demonstrating the feasibility of building advanced semiconductor expertise and talent within the country.
